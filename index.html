Cows

Cows have strong social bonds, often forming close friendships and displaying emotions through vocalizations.

In industrial farming, cows endure confined spaces, with dairy cows repeatedly impregnated and separated from their calves.

Pigs

Pigs are intelligent and curious animals, with cognitive abilities similar to dogs and young children.

Factory farming subjects pigs to overcrowding and stress, with little room for natural behaviors and social interaction.

Fish

Many fish exhibit complex social structures, showing cooperation, and some species use tools to gather food.

Industrial fishing practices lead to overfishing, disrupting marine ecosystems and causing harm to fish populations.

Chickens

Chickens are social birds with a complex range of vocalizations, used to communicate within the flock.

In many factory farms, chickens are kept in tightly packed conditions, impacting their health and welfare.
